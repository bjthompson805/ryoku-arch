Ryoku art pipeline (beta18)

Model: fal-ai/recraft/v3/text-to-image, style "vector_illustration/line_art".
Key: ~/.config/fal/key. Returns an SVG, not a raster.

Why this and not flux: the art has to read as a minimal instrument mark, not an
illustration. flux/dev renders. Ask it for "greek marble bust" and it builds a
3d sculpture and lights it, whatever style words follow; lead with the medium
and it draws manga, which is still figurative and still anime. recraft's
line_art returns real vector outlines: single weight, no fill, no shading.

The SVG is the point: flat fills, no gradients, so it remaps straight onto the
ramp. No duotone grade, no S-curve, no tonal ceiling to police. The art is
on-palette by construction and stays vector, crisp at any size, ~25KB. Qt
renders SVG natively.

Two traps, both of which cost a pass:

1. Every generation picks its OWN greys. One file came back with linework at
   rgb(57,58,58), another at rgb(0,0,0), another at rgb(96,95,100). Matching
   fills by string silently misses whatever it did not predict, and the missed
   fill stays near-black and invisible. Bucket by luminance instead:

       L > 0.75  -> #000000  paper
       L < 0.35  -> #cdc4ba  ink
       otherwise -> #7a756e  inkFaint

   bin/art/regrade-svg.py does exactly this. Use it, do not hand-map.

2. The light fill is not only the background. It is painted inside every
   outlined shape, over the linework. Map it to `none` and every outline
   collapses into a solid silhouette. Map it to paper.

Prompt grammar:

    <subject>, reduced to pure single-weight outlines, extremely minimal,
    white lines on pure black, no fill, no shading, no texture

Subjects stay Greek and Japanese: a helmeted profile, a coiled dragon, a
blindfolded figure with scales, a plum branch, a torii, a sun disc. Keep one
subject per mark. The style does the work; a long subject clause fights it.

Verify, do not eyeball. Rasterise and measure: chromatic pixels must be 0.00%,
and the ink fraction should sit near 6-17% (a mark, not a picture). Anything
above ~20% midtone means it is being rendered rather than drawn.

The old flux grammar is gone. It produced anime.
